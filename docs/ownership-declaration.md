## 2. Assets owned

The Owner is the sole and exclusive legal and beneficial owner of all rights, title,
and interest in the following ("the Assets"):

a. **Domain name** — `studiq.org`, together with any associated subdomains and any
   variant or related domains registered by or for the Owner.

b. **Brand** — the name "StudiQ", the AI tutor persona "SPOK", associated logos,
   trade dress, and any registered or unregistered trade marks.

c. **Software** — the StudiQ web application source code, including all front-end,
   back-end, database schema/migrations, and associated configuration, in every
   version and repository.

d. **Content & data** — generated lessons, questions, curriculum mappings, prompts,
   and all other proprietary content, excluding third-party content used under
   licence (e.g. exam-board specifications, third-party libraries).

e. **Intellectual property** — all copyright, database rights, design rights,
   trade marks, and any other intellectual property rights subsisting in (a)–(d),
   worldwide, for the full term of those rights.

## 3. Declaration

The Owner declares that:

1. The Owner created and/or lawfully acquired the Assets and holds full title to them.
2. The Assets are not subject to any undisclosed lien, charge, licence, or
   encumbrance, except as listed in **Schedule A**.
3. To the best of the Owner's knowledge, the Assets do not infringe the rights of
   any third party.
4. Any contributions made to the Software by contractors, employees, or AI tools
   were made on a work-for-hire / assignment basis, with all resulting IP assigned
   to the Owner (see **Schedule B**).

## 4. Third-party components

The Software incorporates open-source and third-party components under their
respective licences (e.g. Next.js, Supabase client libraries, mathjs). This
Declaration does not claim ownership of those components, only of the StudiQ
application and original content built with them.

## 5. Governing law

This Declaration is governed by the laws of **England and Wales** and the courts of
England and Wales have exclusive jurisdiction.
